subroutine, public metdisaggmodule::disagggridcell ( real, intent(in)  longitude,
real, intent(in)  latitude,
real, intent(in)  delt 
)

Main subroutine to disaggregate input meteorology to that of the physics timestep.

Parameters
[in]deltSimulation physics timestep

First check that we should be doing the disaggregation. If we are already at the needed timestep (delt) then we can return to the main.

Determine how many physics timesteps fit into a metInputTimeStep

Determine how many metInputTimeStep fit into a day

Determine the number of physics timesteps that fit in a day

Compute the timestep count (both original timestep and the needed one)

Add two extra days to the vcount since we need them for interpolation of the first and last days of our time period

Expand original MET data to physics timestep. This increases the array size and puts the old values within the larger array. It also duplicates the first and last days for the interpolation. This also expands the time array (metTime)

Perform the interpolations that are either step (for longwave), linear (for for air temperature, specific humidity, wind, pressure), randomly distributed over a number of wet timesteps (precip), or diurnally distributed (shortwave)

Adjust the met arrays for the timezone relative to Greenwich and also trim off the added two days.

Expands the meteorological variable arrays so they can accomodate the amount of timesteps on the physics timestep. Also copies the first day and last day values into the array to give a startday -1 and endday + 1 values for the interpolations. While we are at it we also expand the time array.

Transfer the present contents of the met*** arrays to temp ones.

Allocate the met*** arrays in preparation to fill with old contents the allocated size is now including the met on the physics timestep. The shortwave and precipitation arrays don't require the extra two padding days.

The remainder of the meteorological variables need to have a couple extra days pinned on, one at the start and one at the end.

Now fill the newly expanded arrays with the values you have in the position of their timesteps

Now expand the time dimension and fill in the values.
